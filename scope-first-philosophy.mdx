## The Rule

Before any coding session, the scope must be written down and agreed on. Not in your head. Written.

This is not bureaucracy. It is the single biggest predictor of whether a session produces working software or wasted hours.

## Why It Matters

Every session without a written scope ends the same way: you build the wrong thing, or the right thing three times bigger than needed, or you lose track of what done means and keep going.

A written scope forces you to answer one question before starting: **what is the minimum thing that needs to exist for this session to be a success?**

## The Format

A scope needs three things:

1. **What we are building** — one sentence, present tense, specific
2. **What we are not building** — explicit list, no vagueness
3. **Definition of done** — checkboxes, binary pass/fail

If you cannot write those three things in under 10 minutes, you do not understand the problem yet. Figure that out first.

## For HyperID

Every sprint starts with an updated `scope.mdx` and `build-plan.mdx`. If either is stale, update them before writing code. No exceptions.
